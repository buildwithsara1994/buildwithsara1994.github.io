Evaluating Privacy Perceptions, Experience, and Behavior of Software Development Teams

This project investigates how software development teams understand and implement privacy practices across the Software Development Life Cycle (SDLC). We conducted a large-scale survey with 362 participants from 23 countries to examine developers’ privacy perceptions, practices, and challenges when implementing privacy requirements.

The findings highlight differences in how privacy is understood across roles such as developers, product managers, and security professionals, and emphasize the need for role-aware privacy engineering solutions.

This research was conducted in collaboration with Google Inc., helping ensure industry relevance and practical insights into privacy engineering practices.

Published in the USENIX Symposium on Usable Privacy and Security (SOUPS), Philadelphia, USA, 2024.

Requirements Satisfiability with In-Context Learning

This research explores how large language models can assist software engineers in reasoning about privacy and regulatory requirements. The study evaluates how in-context learning and prompting strategies can determine whether software systems satisfy GDPR consent requirements.

Results demonstrate that modern language models can achieve high accuracy in verifying requirement satisfaction, highlighting their potential as tools for privacy-aware software engineering.

This work was conducted in collaboration with researchers from Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).

Published in the IEEE International Requirements Engineering Conference (RE), 2024.

Patterns of Inquiry in a Community Forum for Legal Compliance with Privacy Law

This study analyzes discussions in Reddit communities to understand how developers interpret and reason about legal privacy requirements such as GDPR.

Through qualitative analysis of forum discussions, the research identifies patterns in how developers structure arguments, present evidence, and reason about compliance with privacy regulations.

This work was conducted in collaboration with researchers from Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).

Published in the IEEE Requirements Engineering Conference Workshops (REW), 2024.

The Role of Online Forums in Developer Understanding of Privacy Law

This project combines survey data from Reddit users with large-scale analysis of privacy-related discussions to understand how practitioners interpret and implement privacy regulations.

The research investigates credibility assessment, ambiguity resolution, and the role of community advice in real-world compliance decisions.

This work was conducted in collaboration with researchers from Carnegie Mellon University (CMU).

Submitted to Proceedings on Privacy Enhancing Technologies (PETS2026).

Privacy Perceptions and Decision-Making in IoT Ecosystems

This ongoing work investigates how users interact with IoT devices and how their privacy perceptions align with real data collection practices in smart home environments.

The study explores user awareness, privacy expectations, and risk perceptions related to smart devices and connected technologies.

This research is conducted in collaboration with researchers from The University of Texas at San Antonio (UTSA).
