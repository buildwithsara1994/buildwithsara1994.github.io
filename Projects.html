Featured Project

PriGen – AI Tool for App Privacy Compliance

PriGen is an AI-based tool designed to help developers identify privacy compliance issues directly from application code. As part of the University of Maine MIRTA Accelerator program, I contributed to customer discovery interviews to understand developer challenges related to privacy compliance and explore opportunities for tool design and commercialization.

Other Projects

Developer Workflow Study

Designed and conducted a large-scale survey with over 300 professionals to understand decision-making processes within software development teams. The study identified patterns in developer workflows, collaboration practices, and challenges in implementing privacy and security requirements in real-world development environments.

Online Community Knowledge Analysis

Analyzed large sets of professional forum discussions to understand how developers evaluate information, interpret privacy regulations, and collaboratively solve technical problems. This work explored how community knowledge sharing shapes decision-making in software engineering and regulatory compliance contexts.
