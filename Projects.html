Featured Project

PriGen – AI Tool for App Privacy Compliance

PriGen is an AI-based tool designed to help developers identify privacy compliance issues directly from application code. As part of the University of Maine MIRTA Accelerator program, I contributed to customer discovery interviews, identifying developer challenges in privacy compliance and exploring opportunities for tool design and commercialization.

Other Projects

Developer Workflow Study

Designed and conducted a large-scale survey with over 300 professionals to understand decision-making processes within software development teams. The study identified patterns in developer workflows, collaboration practices, and common challenges in implementing privacy and security requirements.

Online Community Knowledge Analysis

Analyzed large-scale discussions from professional online forums to understand how people evaluate information, resolve ambiguity, and collaborate to solve technical and regulatory problems. The project focused on identifying patterns in how developers interpret privacy regulations and share knowledge within communities.
