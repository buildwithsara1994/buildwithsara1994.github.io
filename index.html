Sara Haghighi 🐱

I am a PhD candidate in Computer Science at the University of Maine School of Computing and Information Science. My work focuses on understanding user needs, developer workflows, and regulatory requirements to design tools and systems that solve real-world problems.

I work under the supervision of Dr. Sepideh Ghanavati, whose research focuses on privacy engineering and regulatory compliance.

As part of my doctoral research, I conduct studies on how developers interpret privacy regulations, make decisions during software development, and evaluate technical solutions. My work combines user research, data analysis, and AI-assisted tools to identify opportunities for improving developer workflows and privacy compliance.

Since May 2023, I have worked as a Research Assistant at the University of Maine, conducting user studies and data analysis to understand developer practices and translate research insights into opportunities for better tools and systems. Earlier in my doctoral program, I served as a Teaching Assistant from Fall 2022 to May 2023, supporting courses including Programming Languages and Foundations of Computer Science.

Featured Project

PriGen – AI Tool for App Privacy Compliance

PriGen is an AI-based tool designed to help developers identify privacy compliance issues directly from application code. As part of the University of Maine MIRTA Accelerator program, I contributed to customer discovery interviews, identifying developer challenges in privacy compliance and exploring opportunities for tool design and commercialization.
